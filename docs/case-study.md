# Case Study

## Problem

Restaurant websites often show the venue but fail to guide the visitor. A strong site needs to communicate atmosphere, cuisine, trust, booking intent, and location confidence without feeling like a generic template.

## Approach

I treated the site as a hospitality journey:

- lead with atmosphere and premium positioning
- make the menu feel confident without overwhelming the page
- create clear reservation and enquiry intent
- use proof signals without overloading the design
- keep mobile behavior as a first-class concern

## Key Decisions

- Use a visual-first hero rather than a flat brochure layout.
- Keep calls to action direct and visible.
- Organize menu content around confidence and scan speed.
- Treat public data and media carefully.
- Avoid publishing private prototype code as a free source package.

## Outcome

The case study shows the ability to move from public research to an elevated hospitality web direction that a recruiter or hiring manager can understand quickly.
